Nepal's international trade is growing beyond its neighbors, with millions of dollars in exports and imports flowing through the country. To participate in this trade, every business entity must obtain an **Export-Import (EXIM) Code**. This guide explains the registration process and the legal requirements at the Department of Customs in Kathmandu.

## 1. What is an EXIM Code?

An **EXIM Code** is a unique identification number required by any business wishing to import or export goods across Nepal's borders. Without this 13-digit code, the customs clearing system will not allow your goods to pass through.

## 2. Prerequisites for EXIM Registration

Before applying for an EXIM code, your business must have:
- **Registration Certificate:** From the Office of Company Registrar, Department of Commerce, or Department of Industries.
- **PAN/VAT Certificate:** Active registration with the Inland Revenue Office.
- **Bank Guarantee:** A mandatory bank guarantee from a commercial bank (the amount is subject to periodic government updates, currently around **NPR 300,000** for importers).

## 3. The Registration Procedure in Kathmandu

The application is largely processed through the **Department of Customs (DOC)** online portal, but requires physical verification.

### Steps:
1. **Online Submission:** Fill out the EXIM application on the Department of Customs website.
2. **Document Upload:** Upload scanned copies of your citizenship/passport, company registration, and bank guarantee.
3. **Internal Review:** The Department reviews the documents for tax compliance and legal standing.
4. **Code Issuance:** Once approved, your 13-digit EXIM code is issued, which is typically linked to your PAN number.

## 4. Key Documents Required

- **Company Registration Certificate.**
- **Memorandum and Articles of Association.**
- **PAN/VAT Certificate.**
- **Recommendation from a Commercial Bank.**
- **Original Copy of the Bank Guarantee.**
- **Citizenship/Passport of the Authorized Person.**

## 5. Annual Compliance & Renewal

An EXIM code is not a "once-and-for-all" registration. It must be **renewed annually** simultaneously with your Department of Commerce or Industry renewal. Failure to renew will result in the immediate blocking of your import/export privileges at all custom points.
