For international corporations looking to expand their presence in Nepal without establishing a new subsidiary, registering a **Branch Office** is an excellent alternative. Under the **Companies Act, 2063**, foreign companies can legally operate in Nepal through a registered branch. This guide explores the registration procedure and compliance requirements at the Office of the Company Registrar in Kathmandu.

## 1. Branch Office vs. Liaison Office

Before registering, it is important to understand the difference between these two entities:
- **Branch Office:** Can engage in commercial activities, earn revenue, and enter into contracts within Nepal.
- **Liaison Office:** Primarily used for information gathering and coordination. A liaison office **cannot** generate income or conduct any commercial business in Nepal.

## 2. The Registration Process

The registration of a branch office is handled by the **Office of the Company Registrar (OCR)** in Kathmandu.

### Step 1: Document Preparation
The parent company must prepare resolutions and powers of attorney authorizing the branch setup. All foreign documents must be notarized or apostilled (depending on the country) and translated into Nepali.

### Step 2: Application Submission
Submit the application to the OCR including the parent company's registration documents, financial statements, and the proposed activities of the branch in Nepal.

### Step 3: Approval and Registration
If the OCR is satisfied with the documentation and the proposed activities, they will issue the **Branch Registration Certificate**.

## 3. Post-Registration Compliance

Once the branch is registered, it must adhere to several local laws:
- **Tax Registration:** Obtain a **PAN/VAT** number from the Inland Revenue Office.
- **Local Permits:** Register with the local Ward office and, if required, the Department of Industries.
- **Reporting:** Submit annual financial statements of the parent company and the local branch to the OCR.

## 4. Key Documents Required

- **Parent Company Registration Certificate.**
- **Charter/Constitutional Documents (AOA/MOA).**
- **Board Resolution** to open a branch in Nepal.
- **Power of Attorney** for the local representative in Kathmandu.
- **Financial Statements** of the parent company from the previous year.

## 5. Repatriation of Profits

One of the main advantages of a branch office is the ability to repatriate profits made in Nepal back to the parent company. This process must follow the regulations of the **Nepal Rasta Bank (Central Bank)** and usually requires proof of tax payment.
