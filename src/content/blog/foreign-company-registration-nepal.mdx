Nepal has become an increasingly attractive destination for foreign investors, offering a strategic gateway between China and India. However, navigating the legal landscape for **foreign company registration in Nepal** requires precision and professional legal guidance. This guide outlines the essential steps to successfully register your business in Kathmandu.

## 1. Legal Framework: Understanding FITTA

All foreign investments in Nepal are primarily governed by the **Foreign Investment and Technology Transfer Act (FITTA), 2075 (2019)**. The Department of Industries (DOI) is the primary regulatory body that approves your investment proposal.

## 2. Step-by-Step Registration Process

### Step A: Approval from Department of Industries (DOI)
Foreigners must first obtain approval from the DOI (or the Investment Board of Nepal for large projects). You must submit a detailed project report and proof of financial capability.

### Step B: Name Reservation
Once DOI approval is secured, you must reserve your company name at the **Office of the Company Registrar (OCR)** in Kathmandu.

### Step C: Registration at OCR
After name approval, you submit the Memorandum of Association (MOA) and Articles of Association (AOA). This is a critical legal step where the presence of a professional law firm is highly recommended.

### Step D: Ward & Tax Registration
Post OCR registration, the company must:
1. Register with the local Ward Office in Kathmandu.
2. Obtain a Permanent Account Number (PAN) or Value Added Tax (VAT) certificate from the Inland Revenue Office.

## 3. Minimum Investment Requirements

As of 2026, the minimum investment threshold for foreign investors has been adjusted to encourage diverse industries. Generally, it is currently set at **NPR 20 Million** (approx. USD 150,000), though this can vary for specific sectors like Hydropower or Large-scale Manufacturing.
